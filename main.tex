The complete dataset of 1500 cars was split into a 1000-car training set and a 500-car test set. We continue this analysis with the training set.


\subsection*{Explorative analysis \& outlier removal}
\label{outlier_removal}

All variables were examined for univariate outliers. As shown in \cref{extreme_outliers}, both \var{noise level} and \var{nox emissions} contain an extreme outlier. These are the \car{Volkswagen Jetta (from NOV 06 Wk 45 >) -- 1.4 TSI (170 PS) Sport} with a \var{noise level} of 0.3, and the \car{Vauxhall Signum MY2008 -- 3.0CDTi V6 24v with 16/17/18" wheel} with a \var{nox emissions} value of 237000, respectively. They were removed from the training set.

Note the very strong correlations between the \var{urban metric}, \var{extra urban metric}, \var{combined metric}, and \var{co2} variables; and to a lesser extend the \var{engine capacity} variable. This is in accordance with their meaning: the three ``metrics'' are all a measure of fuel consumption. And cars with larger engine volumes consume more liters of fuel, and every liter of fuel contains a fixed amount of $\mathrm{CO_2}$.
